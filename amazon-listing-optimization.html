Amazon Title Optimization Checklist

Your title is the single most important ranking factor. Here's what every optimised Amazon title should include:

  • Brand name (if it helps buyers find you)
  • Primary keyword — the exact phrase buyers search for most
  • Key product feature or model number
  • Secondary keyword or use case
  • Target audience if relevant (e.g. "For Women", "Kids Age 3–8")
  • Size, colour, or pack count if buyers filter by it
  • 150–200 character length — use every character

Bullet Point Best Practices

💥

Lead With a Benefit

Start each bullet with the outcome your buyer gets — not the feature. "STAYS COLD 24 HOURS" beats "Double-wall insulated".

🔠

Bold Keyword First

Amazon shows the first few words in bold on mobile. Put your key phrase there — DOUBLE-WALL INSULATED, LEAK-PROOF LID, etc.

📐

Include Specs

Buyers need exact dimensions, weights, compatibility, and materials before they buy. Include them in the relevant bullet.

🎯

Address Objections

Use your 5th bullet to preempt the most common customer concern — warranty, returns, fit, compatibility.

Backend Keywords — The Hidden Ranking Factor

Amazon gives you 249 characters of backend keywords — search terms invisible to buyers but indexed by the A9 algorithm. Use them for:

  • Synonyms and alternate spellings (colour/color, grey/gray)
  • Competitor brand terms buyers might also search
  • Long-tail phrases that don't fit in your title
  • Common misspellings of your product name
  • Related use cases ("camping water bottle", "gym flask")
